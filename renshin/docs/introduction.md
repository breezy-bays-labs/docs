# Renshin (練心)

Renshin is a personal knowledge vault — the **thinking layer** in a four-tool development workflow.

## The Suite

| Tool | Kanji | Meaning | Role |
|------|-------|---------|------|
| Tankyu | 探究 | Inquiry | Discover external knowledge |
| **Renshin** | **練心** | **Forged Mind** | **Refine, connect, brainstorm** |
| Kata | 型 | Form | Execute with methodology |
| Soji | 掃除 | Tidying | Clean and maintain |

## How It Works

Renshin uses a three-space architecture to separate thinking from operations:

- **self/** — Persistent identity. Who you are, how you think, what you're focused on.
- **notes/** — The knowledge graph. Connected insights organized in topic hierarchies with wiki-links.
- **ops/** — Temporal state. Session logs, processing queues, health reports.

Raw ideas enter through an **inbox**, get processed through a **6-step refinement pipeline** (Record → Reduce → Reflect → Reweave → Verify → Rethink), and emerge as connected knowledge in the graph.

## The Knowledge Flow

```
External World → Tankyu (research) → Renshin (thinking) → Projects (building)
```

Tankyu discovers what's out there. Renshin processes it into insight. Projects apply the insight.

Renshin also serves as a standalone brainstorming space — not everything needs to come from external research. Some of the best ideas emerge from connecting existing notes in unexpected ways.

## The Name

練心 (Renshin) means "forged mind" or "tempered mind." In budō, while kata (型) trains the external form, renshin (練心) forges the internal mind through that practice. The name reflects what the vault does: takes raw thinking and refines it through deliberate, structured processing — tempering ideas until they're sharp and connected.
