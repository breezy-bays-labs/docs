# Accessibility And Quality

## Accessibility Baseline

Accessibility is treated as a design constraint from the start.

Public expectations include:

- sufficient contrast for readable text
- keyboard-operable interfaces
- visible focus states
- meaningful labeling and semantics
- logical heading structure

## Quality Posture

Quality is enforced through a mix of automation and review.

The goal is to automate repeatable checks so human review can focus on judgment, taste, and domain fit.

## What Good Looks Like

- interfaces feel consistent
- states are complete
- errors are recoverable
- empty and loading states are intentional
- interaction feedback is immediate and clear

## Review Philosophy

We care about more than visual polish.

We also care that the interface:

- supports the primary task quickly
- reduces ambiguity
- avoids unnecessary complexity
- feels coherent from screen to screen
